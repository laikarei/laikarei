FUNA is an exploration of sound as a living, relational medium, where memory, desire, and ecological forces converge. The project inhabits what Rei calls the “absolute after”—the open, persistent terrain left by collapse, where absence and residue hold their own presence. Each work unfolds in its erosion or renewal, attending to what remains while allowing new forms to emerge. Rooted in Latin American, Chilean, and Mapuche histories, FUNA transforms the remnants of silenced systems into resonance. Using granular synthesis, spectral processing, and EVP field recordings, loops and glitches are embraced as compositional matter. Listening becomes an embodied encounter with persistence, decay, and transformation.
